Scope

Provides financial cover to the insured in the event of a breach of warranty or a claim under the tax covenant. The policy covers unknown risks and exclusions including identified risks, transfer pricing, secondary tax liabilities and pensions underfunding.

Policyholder

The buyer or seller. Although the seller will often introduce insurance to the deal, the buyer is typically the policyholder (91% of deals). A buyer-side policy allows the buyer to claim directly against the insurer for a breach of warranty or claim under the tax covenant. There is no need to involve the seller in the claim and the insurer will waive subrogation rights against the seller (save in the event of seller fraud).

Time period

The policy periods typically match or, in the case of a buyer-side policy, extend the time limitations set out in the acquisition agreement. The policy period is generally 18-36 months for general warranties and 7 years for title, capacity and tax.

Premium

This is calculated as a function of the policy limit. The premium will depend on factors including jurisdiction of the acquisition agreement, nature and location of the target’s operations and policy parameters. Premium rates are typically 0.75-1.3% for real estate transactions and 1-2% for operational businesses.
